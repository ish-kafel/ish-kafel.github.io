Chapter II _ Present

The Choices We Face

"WHERE CAN I GO"



Along the way a lot has changed and a lot of moments passed; things that felt unusual and exciting before seemed to have lost their novelty a little bit, and other things became more apparent, like "Holy s*** - we're running a company, how do we do that? Do I try to do something with all these opportunities that are coming my way or do I focus on making music like I have till now?
Do we want to tour more or less? How much time would we have left to create stuff? Do we play other people's music or try building a show around our own?"
It was a lot to get used to.

I was often overstimulated and therefore there were a lot of moments where I felt lost, out of touch and lacking control over myself and my surroundings.
But at the same time I fully realized that this was the life I still wanted to live and that all the great and inspiring moments like interacting with people fans, crowds or just random encounters in real life, but also appreciative moments when I was on the road working on music I felt connected to, definitely weighed up to the lesser moments.

Therefore in a lot of ways this EP is about that question of "How do I live with the life I want to live, and which choices are the right ones to do so?"
I felt like I needed to grow in a lot of ways, and realizing that was the first step, but I'm getting ahead of myself again.


_ V

The Choices We Face kind of came at a time where I kind of felt lost. It wasn't necessarily lost due to a lack of direction, but I guess more so lost due to the abundance of possibilities.

With The Choices We Face being the second EP that we were gonna put out it felt like we had to make some sort of a statement. We weren't the new kids on the block anymore, this wasn't our first rodeo. And I think that, at the time, that feeling was kind of haunting us.
Together with it being the second bigger project that we were coming out with, it also came at a time where things on the business end of DROELOE were moving rapidly. We were really running a business at that point, and that was something that was completely foreign to us as two kids who met in art school and decided to start a project together without even thinking of what it could become.

I think something that I do often and that we do with DROELOE as a project is to kind of run with how we're feeling about things and what we think about certain things at the time - and take those feelings and ideas and pour them into our work. And I think this EP is a prime example of that.

Creating this EP was kind of a cathartic experience for me, and I'd like to think that I hold this EP and the timeframe around it so dearly, because it stands as a testament to us finding a way to deal with the hardships and uncertainties and turn it into something of meaning.
